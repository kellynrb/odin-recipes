Pot Roast

Description

A great winter meal to enjoy any time of year. I am Martha Stewart.

Gets better over time. I just finished all the pot roast I had and now I'm sad.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 lb beef pot roast
  • Better than Bouillon beef base
  • Dry red wine
  • Baby carrots
  • Onion
  • 1 box of white mushrooms
  • 1 large can of whole tomatoes
  • 3-5 russet potatoes peeled, cut up into 1-2 inch cubes
  • Fresh ground black peppercorn
  • Dried thyme
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Water
  • Vegetable or canola oil

Steps

  1. Trim beef of any tough looking fat.
  2. Cover a plate with all purpose flour and crack a bunch of black pepper into the flour.
  3. Dredge the meat into the flour, covering all surfaces.
  4. Heat 2-3 tbs of vegetable or canola oil in large soup pot.
  5. Brown the beef in the hot oil on all sides.
  6. Turn the meat to get it nice and browned all over.
  7. Remove meat and put back on plate to rest.
  8. Scrape up the bits in the pan. Remove only burnt bits, leave rest for flavor.
  9. Add a bit more oil and add about 1 1⁄2 cup of coarsely chopped up onions.
  10. Add about 2 cups of baby carrots and sautee in oil, stirring occasionally till browned.
  11. Add 1 box of cleaned mushrooms chopped in half.
  12. Sautee vegetables.
  13. Add 2 crushed garlic cloves and continue to sautee the vegetables together for about 10 minutes on low-medium heat.
  14. Place roast on top of vegetables.
  15. Add 1 large can of whole tomatoes and 1 1⁄2 cups of red wine.
  16. Add 3 cups of water to cover the roast and 4 tsp of beef bouillon paste.
  17. Add crushed black pepper and bay leaves.
  18. Simmer on low heat for about 3-4 hours until the meat is fork tender.
  19. In the last hour add peeled, quartered potatoes. Cook until fork tender.
  20. Serve with crusty french bread and salad.
